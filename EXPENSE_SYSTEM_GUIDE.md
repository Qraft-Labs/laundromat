# Expense Management System - User Guide

    ## What Are Expenses?

    Expenses are **ad-hoc operational costs** - money that leaves the business for day-to-day operations. These are **NOT salaries or regular payroll**. Think of expenses as unexpected or irregular payments needed to keep the business running.

    ## How It Works

    ### 1. Recording an Expense (Any User)
    When something happens that requires payment:
    1. Click **"Record Expense"** button
    2. Fill in details:
    - **Date**: When the expense occurred
    - **Category**: Choose the right category
    - **Description**: Be specific! "Boda boda to pick up supplies from supplier X"
    - **Amount**: How much was paid
    - **Payment Method**: Cash, Mobile Money, Bank Transfer, or Card
    - **Paid To**: Who received the money? "Boda driver John", "Welder at corner shop"
    - **Receipt Number**: If you got a receipt
    - **Notes**: Any additional context
    3. Click **"Record Expense"**
    4. Status is **PENDING** (yellow badge)

    ### 2. Admin Approval
    Only administrators can approve expenses:
    1. Admin sees all **PENDING** expenses
    2. Reviews details (who recorded it, what it was for, amount)
    3. Clicks **✓ Approve** or **✗ Reject**
    4. Once **APPROVED** (green badge):
    - It CANNOT be edited or deleted
    - It affects the financial reports
    - Profit is reduced by this amount

    ### 3. Financial Impact
    When an expense is approved:
    - **Total Expenses** increases
    - **Net Profit** decreases (Revenue - Expenses)
    - **Daily financial summary** updates automatically
    - Shows in Financial Dashboard charts

    ## Best Practices

    ### ✅ DO:
    - **Be specific** in descriptions: "Boda boda to pick up 5 boxes of detergent from Kikuubo" instead of just "Transport"
    - **Record immediately** after the expense happens
    - **Keep receipts** and enter receipt numbers
    - **Track who you paid**: "Welder John from corner shop" not just "Welder"
    - **Add context in notes**: Why was this expense necessary?

    ### ❌ DON'T:
    - Record salaries here (use payroll system)
    - Approve your own expenses (admin approval required)
    - Edit expenses after approval (they're locked for audit trail)
    - Use vague descriptions: "Miscellaneous" or "Various"

    ## Financial Dashboard

    Administrators can see:
    - **Total Revenue**: Money coming in from customers
    - **Total Expenses**: All approved expenses
    - **Net Profit**: Revenue - Expenses (how much you actually made)
    - **Daily trends**: Line chart showing profit over time
    - **Expense breakdown**: Pie chart showing where money is going
    - **Pending approvals**: How many expenses need review

    ## Accountability & Tracking

    Every expense records:
    - **Who recorded it**: Which staff member entered the expense
    - **When recorded**: Date and time
    - **Who approved it**: Which administrator approved/rejected
    - **When approved**: Approval timestamp
    - **All details**: Complete audit trail

    This ensures:
    - No unauthorized expenses
    - Clear responsibility
    - Easy auditing
    - Financial transparency

    ## Example Scenario

    **Monday Morning:**
    1. **Sarah (Cashier)** records: "Boda boda to pick up urgent delivery from customer" - 10,000 UGX
    - Status: PENDING (yellow)
    - Recorded by: Sarah
    
    2. **John (Admin)** reviews and approves
    - Status: APPROVED (green)
    - Today's profit reduced by 10,000 UGX
    
    3. **Financial Dashboard** updates:
    - Total Expenses: +10,000
    - Net Profit: Revenue - 10,000
    - Shows in Transport category

    **End of Day:**
    - All expenses approved
    - Financial report shows exact profit after expenses
    - Clear record of who spent what and why

    ## Tips for Administrators

    1. **Review daily**: Check pending expenses at end of each day
    2. **Ask questions**: If description is vague, ask for clarification before approving
    3. **Watch for patterns**: If transport expenses are high, consider alternatives
    4. **Compare periods**: Use Financial Dashboard to track spending trends
    5. **Export reports**: Download financial reports for accounting

    ## Reporting

    View expense reports by:
    - **Date range**: Last 7 days, this month, this year
    - **Category**: See all transport expenses, all repairs, etc.
    - **Status**: View pending, approved, or rejected
    - **Search**: Find specific expenses by description or paid-to name
    - **Who recorded**: Filter by staff member

    ---

    **Remember**: Expenses affect your daily profit. Only approve legitimate business expenses that helped operations!
